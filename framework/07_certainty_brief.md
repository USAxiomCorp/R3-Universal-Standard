## What a Certainty Brief Is

The R³ Certainty Brief is a structured translation of the primary document — the Dossier or Specification — into the decision language of a specific party who must act on the fixed point.

The primary document is the fixed point of the transaction. It is immutable. It is the source of truth. It belongs to the work itself.

The Certainty Brief is a projection of that fixed point onto the decision space of one specific audience. It changes nothing in the primary. It selects, translates, and frames what that party needs to reach their decision.

```
Challenge the brief       → it resolves to the primary
Challenge the primary     → it resolves to the dissolution audit
Challenge the audit       → it resolves to the axioms
Challenge the axioms      → they resolve to TRUTH_FP

The chain holds.
The fixed point holds.
The certainty is real.
```

### SECTION 1: THE FIXED POINT

*What was found. Plain language. One paragraph maximum.*

State the fixed point in terms the specific audience understands. No jargon from other fields. No hedging. No qualifications beyond what is honest.

```
For a bank:
"This specification identifies [X] as the
optimal configuration of [material/molecule/system].
All industrial compromise has been removed.
What remains is what [X] would have been
if commercialization had never interrupted refinement."

For a regulator:
"This dossier documents the complete methodology
by which [X] was identified as the fixed point
of [domain]. Every assumption was tested.
Every contamination layer was documented.
The result is independently verifiable."

For a court:
"The R³ dissolution audit provides a complete,
documented, independently reproducible record
of how [X] was identified. The chain of reasoning
from axiom to conclusion is unbroken and traceable."
```

---

### SECTION 2: THE PROFIT POINT

*What was frozen. When. Why. Who benefited.*

This section establishes why the fixed point was not found before. It contextualizes the R³ contribution without attacking incumbents.

```
Elements:
─────────────────────────────
What system was frozen
When it was frozen
What triggered the freeze
What was accepted as good enough
What remained unexplored as a result
How long the field has been frozen
```

---

### SECTION 3: THE DISSOLUTION AUDIT SUMMARY

*Not the full audit. A summary appropriate for the audience.*

```
For a bank:        How many contamination layers. 
                   How many removed. Confidence in result.

For a regulator:   What methodology was used.
                   What standards were applied.
                   What independent verification exists.

For a court:       Complete chain of custody of the reasoning.
                   Every step documented and traceable.
                   Expert credentials and methodology basis.

For a scientist:   Full audit available in primary document.
                   Summary of key dissolution decisions.
                   Self-correction record.
```

Always reference the primary document for the complete audit. The brief summarizes. The primary documents.

---

### SECTION 4: VERIFICATION STATUS

*What has been verified. What remains. Timeline and cost.*

This is the most important section for most audiences. Honesty here is non-negotiable.

```
VERIFIED:
─────────────────────────────
[List what has actually been confirmed]
[With method, date, and institution]

NOT YET VERIFIED:
─────────────────────────────
[List what remains to verify]
[With honest statement of uncertainty]

VERIFICATION PATH:
─────────────────────────────
[What needs to happen]
[Who can do it]
[How long it takes]
[What it costs]

VERIFICATION INVITATION:
─────────────────────────────
All results — positive and negative —
will be published openly.
No selective reporting.
The fixed point holds or it does not.
```

## The Standard Certainty Score

Across all industries and all audiences the certainty score uses the same scale:

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **Confirmed** | Fixed point independently verified by 3+ institutions |
| **High** | Fixed point verified by 1-2 independent institutions |
| **Medium-High** | Computationally verified, experimental pending |
| **Medium** | Internal verification complete, independent pending |
| **Pending** | Fixed point specified, verification not yet initiated |
| **Theoretical** | Fixed point derived, no verification performed |

This standardization means any party who receives multiple R³ Certainty Briefs across time and industry can compare certainty levels directly. The score means the same thing for a pharmaceutical brief and a materials brief.

That cross-industry comparability is the product.
