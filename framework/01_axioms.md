## The Seven Axioms

### C1: Everything is composite until proven otherwise.

No system, material, substance, or structure is irreducible until proven so through exhaustive dissolution. What appears simple is always a combination of layers not yet separated.

**Applied to materials:** Every alloy, compound, or processed material contains layers of industrial compromise not yet stripped.

**Applied to finance:** Every credit score contains layers of assumption, bias, and profit-point calibration not yet removed.

**Applied to any industry:** Every product, process, or standard contains layers of accepted compromise not yet questioned.

---

### C2: Gray is black and white not yet separated.

Ambiguity, uncertainty, and complexity are not final states. They are intermediate states awaiting further dissolution. What appears irreducibly complex is two or more distinct things not yet distinguished.

**Applied to materials:** A material that sometimes works and sometimes does not is two materials not yet separated.

**Applied to finance:** A credit score that sometimes predicts and sometimes does not is two populations not yet distinguished.

**Applied to any industry:** Any outcome that is inconsistent is a composite state not yet dissolved.

---

### C3: There is no confusion over uncertainty.

Uncertainty is a property of measurement and knowledge, not of reality. The system being studied is not uncertain. Our description of it may be incomplete. These are different things. Confusion over this distinction is itself contamination.

**Applied to materials:** A material either superconducts at 295 K or it does not. The uncertainty is in our measurement, not in the material.

**Applied to finance:** A person's financial reliability is a fact. The uncertainty is in our ability to measure it, not in the reliability itself.

---

### C4: State change confirms successful dissolution.

When a layer of contamination is successfully removed, the system changes state. State change is the observable signal that dissolution occurred. No state change means the layer was not real contamination or was not fully removed.

**Applied to materials:** A phase transition confirms that a structural layer was dissolved. The system moved to a new state.

**Applied to finance:** An improved score after connecting gig history confirms that a layer of invisibility was dissolved.

---

### C5: Amplification compounds across layers.

Each successful dissolution amplifies the signal of the underlying truth. Contamination suppresses the signal. Removal of contamination does not just restore the signal — it amplifies it, because adjacent layers also begin to dissolve in response.

**Applied to materials:** Removing processing contamination does not just reveal the base material — it reveals a more ordered structure than expected because strain and defect layers dissolve together.

**Applied to finance:** Connecting one data source does not just add a data point — it changes the entire score profile because the new data illuminates previously ambiguous signals.

---

### C6: Reflection reveals hidden structure.

The system contains information about itself. Applying the system's own output as input — reflecting the output back through the system — reveals structure that is invisible in a single pass. Recursion is reflection made formal.

**Applied to materials:** Running the simulation output back through the simulation reveals attractor states invisible in a single calculation.

**Applied to finance:** Running a person's score history back through the scoring algorithm reveals trajectory and reliability signals invisible in a point estimate.

---

### C7: All layers are connected.

No layer is isolated. Every layer of contamination interacts with every other layer. Removing one layer changes the conditions for all others. The system is a whole, not a stack of independent parts. This means dissolution must be iterative — each pass reveals new layers made visible by previous removals.

**Applied to materials:** Removing chemical contamination changes the strain state. Removing strain changes the electronic structure. Removing electronic structure artifacts reveals the phonon spectrum. All connected.

**Applied to finance:** Removing bureau-only dependence changes what gig data means. Changing what gig data means changes how payment history is weighted. All connected.
