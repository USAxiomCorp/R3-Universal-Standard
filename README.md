## What This Is

The Russell Recursive Refinement Standard (R³) is a universal methodology that replaces the entire R&D process — from first principles identification through regulatory-ready specification — by systematically stripping every layer of industrial compromise, assumed requirement, and profit-point calibration from any system in any industry until only the irreducible fixed point remains. Built on seven foundational axioms and operationalized through Composite State Dissolution, R³ does not improve existing systems — it finds what those systems would have been if commercialization had never interrupted refinement, producing specific, falsifiable, verifiable fixed point specifications that are dedicated to the public domain and open to verification by any qualified party worldwide.

---

## The Standard Applied

The open-source superconductor repositories are the first full implementation of R³ applied to materials science — six superconductor specifications derived entirely through Composite State Dissolution of materials that were frozen at their respective profit points, each accompanied by a complete synthesis protocol, theoretical framework, and verification criteria ready for any equipped laboratory to test. Three of those specifications — palladium hydride at 9 K, iron pnictides at 78 K through optoelectronic doping, and armchair carbon nanotubes at 15 K — represent fixed points where the profit point has been precisely identified, the contamination layers have been dissolved, and the path to experimental confirmation requires only execution of the protocol, demonstrating that R³ is not a theoretical framework but a working engine that produces real, testable, open science.
